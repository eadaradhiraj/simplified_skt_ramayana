Sanskrit

Ascetic Valmiki enquired of Narada, preeminent among the sages ever engaged in the practice of religious austerities or study of the Vedas and best among the eloquent.

तपस्वी वाल्मीकिः, तपसि स्वाध्याये निरतं वाग्विदां वरं मुनिपुङ्गवं नारदं पृष्टवान्।

"Who in this world lives today endowed with excellent qualities, prowess, righteousness, gratitude, truthfulness and firmness in his vows?

"साम्प्रतं कः लोके अस्मिन् गुणवान् वीर्यवान् धर्मज्ञः कृतज्ञः सत्यवाक्यः दृढव्रतः।

Who is that one gifted with good conduct, given to the wellbeing of all living creatures, learned in the lore (knowledge of all things that is known), capable of doing things which others can not do and singularly handsome?

चारित्रेण कः युक्तः? सर्वभूतेषां कः हितः? विद्वान् कः? कः समर्थः च? कः च एकप्रियदर्शनः?

Who (among men) is selfrestrained? Who has conquered anger? Who is endowed with brilliance and free from envy? Who is that when excited to wrath even the devatas, are afraid of (let alone foes)?

आत्मवान् कः? कः जितक्रोधः द्युतिमान् अनसूयक:? युद्धे कस्मात् जातरोषात् बिभ्यति देवाः?

O Maharshi, I intend to hear about such a man whom you are able to place? Indeed great is my curiosity".

महर्षे, एतत् श्रोतुम् इच्छामि अहम्। मम कौतूहलं परम्। एवंविधं नरं ज्ञातुं त्वं समर्थः असि।

On listening all those words of Valmiki, Narada, the knower of all the three worlds, said "let it be heard..." and beckoning at Sage Valmiki to listen attentively, he spoke these words very gladly.

त्रिलोकज्ञः प्रहृष्टः नारदः वाल्मीकेः एतत् वचः श्रुत्वा, "श्रूयताम्" इति आमन्त्त्र्य वाक्यम् अब्रवीत्।

"O sage rare indeed are men endowed with the many qualities you have described. I ascertained one. Listen carefully.

मुने, बहवः गुणाः दुर्लभाः एव ये त्वया कीर्तिताः। तैः युक्तं नरं बुद्ध्वा वक्ष्यामि अहम्। श्रूयताम्।

People have heard his name as Rama, who was born in the race of king Ikshvaku, having steady nature, possessing incomprehensible prowess, selfeffulgent, selfcommanding and subjecting senses under his control.

इक्ष्वाकुवंशप्रभवः रामः नाम जनैः श्रुतः, नियतात्मा, महावीर्यः, द्युतिमान्, धृतिमान्, वशी,

He (Sri Rama) is a great intellectual, adherent to rules, eloquent, handsome, destroyer of foes (sins), broadshouldered, strongarmed, having conchshaped neck and prominent cheeks.

बुद्धिमान्, नीतिमान्, वाग्मी, श्रीमान्, शत्रु-निबर्हणः, विपुलांसः, महा-बाहुः, कम्बु-ग्रीवः, महा-हनुः,

Possessing a broad chest, armed with a great bow, with fleshy collar bones, knee-long arms, a noble head, a graceful forehead and great prowess, he is the destroyer of foes (sins).

महा-उरस्कः, महा-इष्वासः, गूढ-जत्रुः, अरिन्दमः, आ-जानु-बाहुः, सुशिराः, सुललाटः, सुविक्रमः,

Mighty and powerful, he has a wellproportioned body, neither tall nor short, shining complexion, well-developed chest, large eyes, lustrous body and good qualities.

समः, सम-विभक्त-अङ्गः, स्निग्धवर्ण:, प्रतापवान्, पीनवक्षा, विशालाक्षः, लक्ष्मीवान्, शुभ-लक्षणः,

Pious, firm in his vows, he is ever intent on doing good to his subjects. He is, illustrious, wise, and pure at heart. He is obedient to elders (or accessible to those who are dependent on him) and ever meditating (on the means of protecting those who take refuge in him).

धर्मज्ञः, सत्यसन्धः, प्रजानां च हिते रतः, यशस्वी, ज्ञानसम्पन्नः, शुचिः, वश्यः, समाधिमान्,

Auspicious like Brahma, Sri Rama is the sustainer of this world, destroyer of enemies and protector of all living beings and of the moral code.

प्रजापति-समः, श्रीमान्, धाता, रिपु-निषूदनः, रक्षिता जीवलोकस्य, धर्मस्य परिरक्षिता,

He has performed the duties of a king and protected his subjects. He is knowledgeable in the true nature of the Vedas. He is a great archer.

रक्षिता स्वस्य, धर्मस्य स्वजनस्य च रक्षिता, वेद-वेदाङ्ग-तत्त्वज्ञः, धनुर्वेदे च निष्ठितः,

He is the knower of the meaning and essence of all the scriptures, excellent at memory, a brilliant one, and dear to all the worlds, gentle, level-headed and clear-headed in discriminating and distinguishing...

सर्व-शास्त्रार्थ-तत्त्वज्ञः, स्मृतिमान्, प्रतिभानवान्, सर्व-लोक-प्रियः साधुः अदीनात्मा, विचक्षणः,

Like an ocean that is reached by rivers, that reverential one too is always accessible and reachable by clean-minded ones, and he treats all equally, and ever a feast to eye...

सर्वदा अभिगतः सद्भिः, समुद्र इव सिन्धुभिः, आर्यः, सर्व-समः च एव, सदा एव प्रियदर्शनः,

He who betters the happiness of his mother Kausalya is an embodiment of all noble merits, and in profundity he is like an unfathomable ocean, and by fortitude he is unalterable like the kingly Himalayan mountain...

सर्व-गुण-उपेत:, कौसल्या-नन्द-वर्धनः, समुद्रः इव गाम्भीर्ये, धैर्येण हिमवान् इव,

In valour Rama is comparable to Vishnu, and in his looks he is attractive like the moon, he equals the earth in his forgiveness, but he is matchable to world ending fire in his wrath...

विष्णुना सदृशः वीर्ये, सोमवत् प्रियदर्शनः, कालाग्नि-सदृशः क्रोधे, क्षमया पृथिवीसमः,

...and in benevolence he is identical to Kubera, God of Wealth, and in his candour he is like Dharma itself, the Truth personified, with none to rival him. That Rama, who was bestowed with all excellent qualities and true prowess...

धनदेन समः त्यागे, सत्ये धर्मः इव अपरः। तम् एवं गुण-सम्पन्नं रामं सत्य-पराक्रमम्

...and who was the eldest and a dear son together with best of qualities, and concerned for welfare of the people was by Dasharatha, with an intent for doing good to the people,

ज्येष्ठं, श्रेष्ठ-गुणैः युक्तं, प्रियं, सुतं, प्रजानां हितैः युक्तं, प्रजा-प्रियकाम्यया दशरथः

Lord of the land, was intended to be installed as Yuvaraja out of love. Then his wife Kaikeyi, who saw his preparation for his coronation,....

महीपतिः यौवराज्येन नियोक्तुम् इष्टवान् प्रीत्या। अथ तस्य अभिषेक-सज्जीकरणं दृष्ट्वा भार्या कैकयी

...the queen, who had been promised earlier with boons, demanded the exile of Rama and enthronement of Bharata.

पूर्वं दत्तवरा देवी वरम् एतम् याचितवती - विवासनं च रामस्य, भरतस्य अभिषेचनम्।

Dasaratha, true to his word and restrained by the bond of duty, sent his beloved son Rama to the forest.

सः धर्म-पाशेन संयतः राजा दशरथः सत्यवचनात् सुतं रामं प्रियं विवासितवान्।

Mighty Sri Rama inorder to please Kaikeyi and obey the word of command of his father, went to the forest and help the king to keep his promise to Kaikeyi.

कैकेय्या: प्रियकारणात्, पितुः वचन-निर्देशात्, सः वीरः प्रतिज्ञामनुपालयन् गतवान् वनम्।

Lakshmana, endowed with humbleness, and augmentor of his mother Sumitra's joy, and a loving and a dear brother to Rama, followed him out of affection...

विनय-सम्पन्नः प्रियः भ्राता सुमित्रा-नन्द-वर्धनः लक्ष्मणः तं व्रजन्तं स्नेहात् अनुगतवान्

...the beloved brother Displaying a brother's fraternal love. Rama's beloved wife always equal to his own life and beneficial to him...

भ्रातरं दयितः भ्रातुः सौभ्रात्रम् अनुदर्शयन्। रामस्य दयिता भार्या नित्यं प्राण-समा हिता

...born in the race of Janaka and daughterinlaw (of Dasharatha), foremost among women...

जनकस्य कुले जाता, देवमाया इव निर्मिता, सर्व-लक्षण-सम्पन्ना, वधूः, नारीषु उत्तमा

...Sita followed him like Rohini followed the Moon. Dasharatha and the citizens followed him far.

यथा रोहिणी शशिनं, सीता अपि अनुगता रामं, पौरैः अनुगतः दूरं, पित्रा दशरथेन च

Rama of righteous nature having approached Guha, king of nishadas, at Shrungiberapura sent back the charioteer

प्रियं निषादाधिपतिं गुहम् आसाद्य धर्मात्मा शृङ्गिबेरपुरे गङ्गाकूले सूतं विसर्जितवान्।

Rama along with Sita, Lakshmana and Guha. They having crossed the rivers which had plenty of water...

लक्ष्मणेन गुहेन सीतया च सहितः रामः। ते वनेन वनं गत्वा बहु-उदकाः नदीः तीर्त्वा

...reached the Chitrakuta mountain by the command of sage Bharadwaja and happily they three having constructed a delightful hut made of leaves in the forest...

चित्रकूटम् अनुप्राप्य भरद्वाजस्य शासनात्, रम्यम् आवसथं कृत्वा रममाणाः वने त्रयः,

...dwelt there happily resembling devas and gandharvas. When Rama had set out to Chitrakuta, king Dasaratha, stricken by the grief over the separation from his son...

देव-गन्धर्व-सदृशाः तत्र ते सुखम् निवसितवन्तः। चित्रकूटं गते रामे पुत्रशोकातुरः तथा

....departed to the heaven due to the grief for this son. After his death Bharata, by the Brahmins led by Vashishtha...

सुतं विलपन् राजा दशरथः स्वर्गं गतवान्। मृते तु तस्मिन् भरतः वसिष्ठ-प्रमुखैः द्विजैः

...the mighty one appointed to rule, did not want the Kingdom. The brave one went to the forest to worship Rama's feet.

नियुज्यमानः महाबलः राज्याय राज्यं न इष्टवान्। सः वीरः गतवान् वनं राम-पाद-प्रसादकः।

Bharata reached Rama, so pleasing venerable, truthful and chivalrous, worshipped him with reverence and implored.

आर्यभावपुरस्कृतः गत्वा तु सुमहात्मानं रामं सत्य-पराक्रमं भ्रातरं राममं याचितवान्।

Bharata addressing Rama said, 'You are knower of righteousness. You alone should be the king'. Rama a source of universal delight, although exceedingly generous and of cheerful countenance, highly renowned and capable. Rama refused to accept the kingdom in accordance with the command of his father.

त्वम् एव राजा धर्मज्ञः इति रामं वचः अब्रवीत्। रामः अपि परम-उदारः सुमुखः सुमहायशाः।

Having handed over his sandals to Bharata as symbol of authority for ruling the kingdom....

न च इष्टवान् पितुः आदेशात् राज्यं रामः महाबलः। न्यासरूपेण राज्याय पादुके भरताय दत्वा, पुनः पुनः...

....Rama persuaded him again and again to return to the capital. His desire being unfulfilled, he touched Rama's sandals....

निवर्तितवान् ततः भरत-अग्रजः (रामः)। सः भरतः इच्छाम् अनवाप्य एव राम-पादौ उपस्पृशन्...

He ruled the kingdom in Nandigram in anticipation of Rama's arrival. After the departure of Bharata, that effulgent and truthfull one...

नन्दिग्रामे अकरोत् राज्यम् राम-आगमन-काङ्क्षया। गते तु भरते श्रीमान् सत्य-सन्धः जितेन्द्रियः...
